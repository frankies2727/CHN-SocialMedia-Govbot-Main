DIVISION XIV
CIVIL LITIGATION ABUSE
Sec. 76. NEW SECTION. 611.24 Civil litigation abuse ——
cause of action.
1. As used in this section:
a. “Civil legal process” means a procedure used in a civil
action, including but not limited to filing a petition, issuing
a subpoena, noticing a deposition, or seeking an injunction,
attachment, or similar relief.
b. “Private party” means an individual, corporation,
partnership, or other legal entity that is not acting on behalf

ns/jh

26/41

of a governmental body.
c. “Ulterior purpose” means using a civil legal process
mainly to obtain a result the civil legal process was not
intended to achieve, including but not limited to coercing
action on an unrelated matter, interfering with employment
or business relationships, or causing financial harm through
harassment.
2. a. This section applies to the use of civil legal
processes by a private party against another private party.
b. This section abrogates the common law cause of action for
abuse of process for actions between private parties.
c. This section does not apply to criminal proceedings or
to actions involving the state, a political subdivision, or an
officer or employee of the state or a political subdivision
acting in an official capacity.
3. A private party may bring a civil action for civil
litigation abuse against another private party who initiated or
caused to be initiated a civil legal process for an ulterior
purpose.
4. To prevail in a civil action brought under this section,
the plaintiff must prove all of the following:
a. The defendant used a civil legal process against the
plaintiff.
b. The civil legal process was used primarily for an
ulterior purpose.
c. The defendant engaged in a specific, willful act in use
of the civil legal process that was not proper in the regular
course of the proceeding.
d. The underlying civil action or proceeding was resolved in
favor of the plaintiff bringing the claim under this section.
5. A private party may bring a civil action under this
section without showing a special or extraordinary injury.
Attorney fees and costs incurred in responding to the misuse of
the civil legal process are sufficient to establish injury.
6. A private party found liable under this section is liable

ns/jh

27/41

for actual damages, as defined in section 714H.2, including
reasonable attorney fees and costs.
Sec. 77. APPLICABILITY. This division of this Act applies
to civil actions or proceedings that are resolved on or after
the effective date of this division of this Act.

APPROPRIATIONS, DISTRIBUTIONS, TRANSFERS, AND EXPENDITURE
AUTHORITY. This bill limits or reduces the standing
appropriations for FY 2026-2027 for payment of claims for
nonpublic school pupil transportation and instructional support
state aid; provides for the use of federal incentive payments
by the department of workforce development for FY 2025-2026
and FY 2026-2027; transfers federal coronavirus aid moneys

ns/jh

36/41

to the health and human services information technology fund
and appropriates moneys in that fund for FY 2026-2027 for
various projects; authorizes salary adjustments to be funded
for FY 2026-2027 as determined by the department of management
from unappropriated moneys in certain special funds; makes
an appropriation from the sports wagering receipts fund each
fiscal year for the double up food bucks program; provides for
expenditure each fiscal year of unencumbered and unobligated
moneys appropriated for the future ready Iowa registered
apprenticeship development program and future ready Iowa
expanded registered apprenticeship opportunities program; and
increases the reduction each fiscal year to state aid for
school district special education support services funding
and appropriates moneys from the reduction beginning in FY
2027-2028.
CORRECTIVE PROVISIONS. The bill makes technical corrections
to, or as the result of, legislation enacted or considered
during the 2026 legislative session. Certain provisions take
effect or apply retroactively based on the underlying 2026
legislation.
NATIONAL ELECTRICAL CODE. The bill adopts certain
provisions of the national electrical code, 2023 edition, with
amendments, as the national electrical code applicable in
this state. The bill prohibits a political subdivision from
adopting a local electrical code that is more restrictive than
the national electrical code. The bill requires the electrical
examining board (board) to conduct an analysis of the cost to
consumers of any significant changes to the national electrical
code prior to adopting any changes to the national electrical
code. The board shall submit the analysis for publication
in the Iowa administrative bulletin along with the notice of
intended action.
The bill repeals the provisions adopting the national
electrical code, 2023 edition, with amendments, as of the
adoption of the national electrical code, 2026 edition,

ns/jh

37/41

published by the national fire protection association, by the
board. The bill requires the board to inform the Iowa Code
editor upon the adoption of the national electrical code,
2026 edition. This division of the bill takes effect upon
enactment.
LOCAL LAWS —— CIVIL RIGHTS. The bill provides that a city
or local government shall not enforce any ordinance or other
law which is broader or has different categories of unfair or
discriminatory practices than those provided in Code chapter
216 (Iowa civil rights Act of 1965).
POLITICAL PARTY STATE CENTRAL COMMITTEES. The bill
prohibits state political party constitutions and bylaws from
prohibiting elected officials from serving on the party’s state
central committee and provides for the election of officers to
the committees.
NOXIOUS WEEDS. The bill declares Japanese knotweed
(Fallopia japonica) to be a primary noxious weed for purposes
of control and eradication.
IOWA STATE FAIR FOUNDATION —— NONRESIDENT DEER HUNTING
LICENSE. The bill provides for one additional nonresident deer
hunting license to be allocated as determined by the Iowa state
fair foundation.
PROPRIETARY TREATMENT SYSTEMS. The bill provides for
inspection, monitoring, and maintenance requirements for
proprietary treatment systems, and requires associated reports.
POULTRY ASSOCIATION. The bill replaces references to
the Iowa poultry association with the north central poultry
association or its successor organization.
HEALTH CARE ACCESS AND INNOVATION TAX CREDIT. The bill
creates a health care access and innovation tax credit for
insurance company taxes for tax years beginning on or after
January 1, 2026.

RESEARCH. The bill provides for appropriations of revenue from

JUDICIAL BRANCH AND COUNTY ATTORNEY SALARIES. Under current
law, the supreme court must establish and may amend a personnel
system and a pay and benefits plan for court employees. The
bill requires the supreme court to also establish a pay
and benefits plan for judicial officers and the state court
administrator in addition to court employees. Under current
law and the bill, the supreme court is required to set the
salary of the state court administrator within pay grade 43 of
the pay plans published by the department of administrative
services (Code section 8A.461).
The bill repeals Code section 602.1501 (judicial salaries),
which provides that the general assembly will set the salary
for the chief justice of the supreme court, each justice of
the supreme court, the chief judge of the court of appeals,
each judge of the court of appeals, the chief judge of each
judicial district, each district judge, each district associate
judge, each full-time associate juvenile judge, each full-time
associate probate judge, and each magistrate. Under current
law, salary rates for such judicial officers are set by 2025
Iowa Acts, chapter 158 (judicial branch appropriations), until

The bill strikes the requirement that the salary of a

200,000 or less be between 45 percent and 100 percent of a
district judge (Code section 331.752(5)).
This division of the bill takes effect June 19, 2026.
CIVIL LITIGATION ABUSE. The bill authorizes private parties

HUMAN TRAFFICKING. The bill substantially applies the

FEDERAL GRANTS AND LOANS —— NOTIFICATION. The bill requires

of the general assembly about executive branch entities or
the judicial branch, respectively, applying for or receiving
certain federal grants or loans.
AREA EDUCATION AGENCIES —— QUARTERLY PAYMENTS —— FY
2026-2027. 2026 Iowa Acts, Senate File 2201, provides for
quarterly, rather than monthly, payments of state aid moneys
to area education agencies beginning in FY 2027-2028. The
bill instead provides that such quarterly payments begin in FY
2026-2027.
HIGHER EDUCATION —— CIVIC PROFICIENCY. The bill provides
that the state board of regents must require each institution
of higher education to establish as a requirement for the
completion of any general education requirements or core
curricula that an undergraduate student complete introductory
survey courses in American history and American government as
provided in the bill. The requirement applies to undergraduate
students beginning enrollment at institutions in academic years
beginning on or after July 1, 2028.
