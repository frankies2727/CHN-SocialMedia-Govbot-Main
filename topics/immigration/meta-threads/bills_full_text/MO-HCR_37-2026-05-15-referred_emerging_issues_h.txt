JOSEPH ENGLER, Chief Clerk

WHEREAS, the Constitution of the United States guarantees due process, equal
protection under the law, and fundamental civil liberties to all persons within the United
States, regardless of citizenship or immigration status; and
WHEREAS, U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) is entrusted with
enforcing immigration law in a manner consistent with the Constitution, federal statutes, and
basic human rights; and
WHEREAS, numerous credible reports, court filings, and testimony from affected
individuals, journalists, attorneys, and human rights organizations have documented patterns
of unlawful detention, racial profiling, harassment, denial of due process, and excessive use
of force by ICE agents; and
WHEREAS, U.S. citizens and lawful residents have been wrongfully detained,
questioned, harmed, or killed during ICE enforcement actions, undermining public trust and
violating constitutional protections; and
WHEREAS, migrants, including undocumented immigrants, asylum seekers, and
mixed-status families, have suffered serious physical harm, psychological trauma, family
separation, and, in some cases, death while in ICE custody or during enforcement operations,
raising grave concerns about accountability and oversight; and
WHEREAS, fear of immigration enforcement has had a chilling effect on community
safety, discouraging individuals from reporting crimes, seeking medical care, attending
school, or participating in civic life; and

WHEREAS, migrants are integral members of communities across the United States,
contributing as workers, caregivers, students, entrepreneurs, artists, and faith leaders; and
WHEREAS, immigrant labor sustains critical sectors of the United States economy,
including agriculture, construction, health care, food service, logistics, and technology, and
migrants collectively contribute billions of dollars annually in taxes, consumer spending, and
economic growth; and
WHEREAS, a humane and just immigration system must center dignity,
accountability, public safety, and respect for human rights rather than fear, punishment,
and collective harm:
NOW THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED that the members of the House of
Representatives of the One Hundred Third General Assembly, Second Regular Session, the
Senate concurring therein, hereby condemn abuses by U.S. Immigration and Customs
Enforcement, affirm the constitutional and human rights of all people within the United
States, and recognize the essential social and economic contributions of migrants to the
nation; and
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Chief Clerk of the Missouri House of
Representatives be instructed to prepare a properly inscribed copy of this resolution for the
members of the Missouri Congressional delegation and Todd Lyons, the senior ICE official.
