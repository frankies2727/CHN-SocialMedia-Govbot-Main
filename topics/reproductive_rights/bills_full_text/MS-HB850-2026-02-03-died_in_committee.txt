HOUSE BILL NO.

AN ACT TO REPEAL SECTIONS 41-41-45 AND 97-3-3, MISSISSIPPI
CODE OF 1972, WHICH PROHIBIT THE PERFORMING OR INDUCING OF AN
ABORTION EXCEPT WHERE NECESSARY FOR THE PRESERVATION OF THE
MOTHER'S LIFE OR WHERE THE PREGNANCY WAS CAUSED BY RAPE; TO
PROVIDE FOR A STATEWIDE SPECIAL ELECTION TO BE HELD ON TUESDAY,
NOVEMBER 3, 2026, FOR THE PURPOSE OF DETERMINING WHETHER ABORTIONS
SHALL BE PROHIBITED IN MISSISSIPPI; TO PROVIDE THAT IF A MAJORITY
OF THE QUALIFIED ELECTORS VOTING ON THE QUESTION IN THE SPECIAL
ELECTION VOTE FOR PROHIBITING ABORTIONS IN MISSISSIPPI, THEN THIS
ACT SHALL NOT TAKE EFFECT; TO PROVIDE THAT IF A MAJORITY OF THE
QUALIFIED ELECTORS VOTING ON THE QUESTION IN THE ELECTION VOTE
AGAINST PROHIBITING ABORTIONS IN MISSISSIPPI, THEN THIS ACT SHALL
TAKE EFFECT ON JANUARY 1, 2027; AND FOR RELATED PURPOSES.

BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI:

SECTION 1.

Sections 41-41-45 and 97-3-3, Mississippi Code of

1972, which prohibit the performing or inducing of an abortion

except where necessary for the preservation of the mother's life

or where the pregnancy was caused by rape, are repealed.

SECTION 2.

There shall be a statewide special election for

the purpose of determining whether Section 1 of this act will take

effect.

general elections are held.
