HJR___ RECOGNIZING THE NEED FOR A NORTH-SOUTH TRANSPORTATION

ROUTE IN EAST ALABAMA.

WHEREAS, there is a strong economic need to have a

four-lane north-south transportation route that connects

East Alabama to the Port of Mobile, particularly due to the

amount of manufacturing in Talladega, Calhoun, and Etowah

Counties; and

WHEREAS, currently, East Alabama only has access to

Interstates 59, 20, and 85, which run east-west; a

north-south route in the eastern counties of Alabama would

be a more direct route to the Port of Mobile and the

transportation of goods; and

WHEREAS, a north-south route would connect the
economies and communities of East Alabama; and
WHEREAS, a north-south route would benefit the

economy of the state overall, including creating another

route that accesses Alabama's beautiful gulf coast, which

will increase tourism and serve to relieve congestion on

I-65 and the other north-south routes in the state; now

therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH

HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That this body recognizes the

necessity to create north-south transportation
