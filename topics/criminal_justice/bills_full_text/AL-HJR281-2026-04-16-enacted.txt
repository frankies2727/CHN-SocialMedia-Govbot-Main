HJR___ URGING THE STATE COMMITTEE OF PUBLIC HEALTH TO REVIEW

AND CONSIDER ADDING THE NOVEL SYNTHETIC OPIOID

N-PROPIONITRILE CHLORPHINE AS A SCHEDULE I CONTROLLED

SUBSTANCE.

WHEREAS, the synthetic opioid known as Cychlorphine

(N-Propionitrile Chlorphine) has emerged as a lethal threat

in the illicit drug market, with in vitro pharmacological

data indicating it is approximately 10 times more potent

than fentanyl; and

WHEREAS, because of its extreme potency, even small

doses of Cychlorphine can cause rapid respiratory depression

and failure, leading to a significantly higher risk of

accidental overdose and death compared to existing

controlled substances; and

WHEREAS, Cychlorphine has been identified by forensic

laboratories in multiple states and has been linked to a

rising number of fatalities, often appearing in counterfeit

pills and/or in combination with other substances without

the user's knowledge; and

WHEREAS, this substance possesses a high potential

for abuse and has no currently accepted medical use in the

United States; now therefore,

BE IT RESOLVED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF ALABAMA, BOTH
HOUSES THEREOF CONCURRING, That we hereby formally request

and direct the State Committee of Public Health to

immediately exercise its administrative authority to review

and consider adding N-Propionitrile Chlorphine

(Cychlorphine) to the list of Schedule I controlled

substances based on the set of factors included in Section

20-2-20 of the Code of Alabama 1975.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, That a copy of this

resolution be transmitted to the State Health Officer of the

Alabama Department of Public Health and the members of the

State Committee of Public Health to ensure the swift

implementation of this process.
