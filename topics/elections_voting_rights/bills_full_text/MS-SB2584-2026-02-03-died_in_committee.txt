SENATE BILL NO. 2584

AN ACT TO CREATE THE "IN-PERSON EARLY VOTING ACT"; TO PROVIDE
LEGISLATIVE FINDINGS AND DEFINITIONS; TO PROVIDE THAT THE
IN-PERSON EARLY VOTING PERIOD SHALL BEGIN 15 DAYS BEFORE THE
ELECTION AND CONTINUE NO EARLIER THAN THE SECOND DAY PRECEDING THE
ELECTION; TO PROVIDE THAT IN-PERSON EARLY VOTING SHALL BE FOR EACH
PRIMARY, GENERAL, RUNOFF, SPECIAL AND MUNICIPAL ELECTION FOR
PUBLIC OFFICE; TO PROVIDE MINIMUM HOURS FOR IN-PERSON EARLY
VOTING, INCLUDING WEEKEND AND EVENING ACCESS; TO PROVIDE THE
PROCEDURES TO FOLLOW WHEN CASTING A BALLOT DURING THE IN-PERSON
EARLY VOTING PERIOD; TO PROVIDE THE MANNER FOR CHALLENGING THE
QUALIFICATIONS OF A VOTER DURING THE IN-PERSON EARLY VOTING
PERIOD; TO PROHIBIT VOTING VIA BALLOT HARVESTING, BALLOT DROP
BOXES OR WITH MAIL-IN OR ABSENTEE BALLOTS FOR REASONS OR INSTANCES
OTHER THAN WHAT IS ALLOWED BY THE MISSISSIPPI ELECTION CODE; TO
AMEND SECTIONS 23-15-625, 23-15-627, 23-15-631, 23-15-637,
23-15-713, 23-15-715 AND 23-15-719, MISSISSIPPI CODE OF 1972, TO
CONFORM; AND FOR RELATED PURPOSES.

BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF MISSISSIPPI:
