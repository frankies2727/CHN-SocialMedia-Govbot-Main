House Resolution 43EX
By: Representatives Santos of the 117th, Roberts of the 52nd, Lim of the 98th, Drenner of the
85th, Hugley of the 141st, and others

A RESOLUTION

Recognizing the growing danger that artificial intelligence deepfakes pose to election

integrity, public trust, and the people of Georgia; and for other purposes.

WHEREAS, artificial intelligence can now generate fabricated images, audio, and video that

are nearly impossible to tell apart from authentic recordings of real people, placing words in

a person's mouth they never spoke and actions on a person they never took; and

WHEREAS, these fabrications, commonly known as deepfakes, pose a direct threat to the

integrity of our elections, the reputations of those who step forward to serve, and the trust of

the voters who depend on the truth to govern themselves; and

WHEREAS, a fabricated image released in the final days of a campaign can reach thousands

of voters before the truth ever has a chance to catch up; and

WHEREAS, candidates and elected officials in this state have already been targeted by

fabricated, AI-generated content created to deceive voters and damage reputations; and

WHEREAS, the same technology that threatens our elections also threatens survivors of

abuse, our seniors, and our children, any of whom can be made to appear to say or do

something they never did; and

WHEREAS, thirty states have already enacted laws addressing the use of deepfakes in

elections, most requiring clear disclosure of AI-generated content in political

communications, and Georgia is not yet among them; and

WHEREAS, the people of Georgia have a right to know whether what they see and hear

about those who seek to serve them is real, and no Georgian, regardless of party, position,

or place, should have their voice stolen and their name dishonored by a machine; and

WHEREAS, the State of Georgia has a compelling interest in protecting the truth, defending

the integrity of its elections, and shielding its citizens from deliberate deception.

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES that

the members of this body encourage the study and enactment of legislation to require clear

disclosure of AI-generated content in political communications and to provide meaningful

recourse for Georgians harmed by deceptive deepfakes in our elections.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of this body recognize the serious and

growing danger that artificial intelligence deepfakes pose to election integrity, public trust,

and the people of Georgia as well as affirm their commitment to protecting Georgia voters

from fabricated and deceptive AI-generated content.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Clerk of the House of Representatives is authorized

and directed to make appropriate copies of this resolution available for distribution to the

public and the press.
